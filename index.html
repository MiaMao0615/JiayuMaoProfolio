A timeline-driven system ensuring spatial consistency and natural human-AI interaction through three core mechanisms.

Timeline-Driven: Virtual human behavior follows a 24-hour schedule
Spatial Consistency: Maintains position across sessions
Natural Entry and Exit: Seamless transitions between real and virtual worlds

Core Mechanisms

Timeline-Driven State and Motion Control

Virtual human follows a 24-hour activity schedule, ensuring consistent behavior patterns aligned with daily routines.

Marker-Based Path Matching and Natural Entry

Uses spatial markers to enable natural entry into the AR space, matching user's physical path with virtual positioning.

Graceful Exit and Spatial Consistency

Maintains spatial memory across sessions, allowing virtual human to gracefully exit and return to the same location.

From Passive Listener to Autonomous Partner

System Overview

An autonomous agent that decides when and what to speak in multi-party conversations, leveraging persona signals, scene context, and dialogue strategy to predict intervention willingness and select appropriate responses.

Research Question: When and what an agent should say in group conversations
Core Method: Multi-turn dialogue corpus · Willingness prediction · Strategy selection
Quantitative Results: Intervention accuracy · Strategy selection accuracy

Core Mechanisms

Persona-Based Willingness Modeling

I model an agent's intervention willingness from persona signals by annotating ~300K instances with continuous scores and fine-tuning Qwen-7B, reducing MAE from ~0.23 to ~0.02.

Scene-Aware Intervention Prediction

I predict intervention willingness from environmental and scene cues by annotating a ~300K scene-level corpus with continuous labels and fine-tuning a regression head that reduces MAE from ~0.26 to ~0.0065.

Topic and Strategy Decision Module

I decide when to speak and how to respond from dialogue content by annotating ~450K instances with insertion points and intervention styles, improving MAE from ~0.21 to ~0.08 and strategy accuracy from 74% to 94%.

Emotion-Aware Bargaining System for Negotiation Training

System Overview

An immersive 3D negotiation platform that captures facial expressions, body movements, and behavioral cues in real-time, enabling multi-modal analysis of the user's negotiation state and adaptive strategy selection.

Problem: Negotiation is multi-dimensional, rarely captured by traditional training methods
System Design: Immersive 3D platform with continuous multi-modal capture and real-time analysis
Strategy Selection: Agent adapts negotiation behavior by analyzing language cues, facial expressions, and bidding patterns

Core Mechanisms

Multi-Modal Input Capture

Continuous real-time capture of facial expressions, body movements, speech patterns, and bidding trajectory to analyze the user's negotiation state.

Emotion-Aware Strategy Selection

The agent selects response strategies and pricing actions by analyzing user language cues, facial expressions, and rapid changes in offered prices.

Body–Language and Speech Consistency Shapes Pricing Outcomes

When facial expressions diverge from bidding behavior, the system adjusts prices; neutral body language with rational language leads to lower offers.

Results and Evaluation

The system demonstrates how different negotiation strategies and outcomes correlate with real-time emotional responses, enabling effective negotiation training under multimodal pressure.
